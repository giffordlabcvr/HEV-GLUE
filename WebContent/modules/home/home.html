HEV-GLUE

Hepatitis E virus (HEV) was first documented in 1955, its taxonomic name is Orthohepevirus A). It is a single-stranded positive-sense RNA virus of the Orthohepevirus genus, in the family Hepeviridae.

In humans, HEV infections cause chronic hepatitis in certain populations. HEV can also cause acute liver disease, which may be severe or fatal in some cases.

This database collates several thousand HEV genome sequences from the literature.
